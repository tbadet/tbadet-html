I completed my PhD in December 2017, after three years working on quantitative genetics in the context of plant microbe interactions at the INRAE institute of Castanet-Tolosan under the supervision of Dr. Sylvain Raffaele. To strengthen my analytical skills and gain experience in a new scientific context, I joined the laboratory of Prof. Daniel Croll, a recognized leading scientist in the field of evolutionary genomics. Implementing genomic and bioinformatic methods to the study of the mechanisms promoting genomic variation in fungi, I successfully led to publication multiple projects as leading scientist while engaging in fruitful collaborations. Since April 2021, after my position as research collaborator in the group of Daniel Croll, I joined the Cell and Molecular Biology laboratory of Prof. Josephus Vermeer as a Maître-Assistant at the University of Neuchâtel, where I develop and teach a phytopathology course at the Bachelor and Master level. I the frame of this 6-years position, I recently applied to a junior researcher position at the National Centre for the Scientific Research (CNRS). The proposed project, currently under evaluation, aims to implement phylogenomic approaches to decipher the evolution of chromosomal mosaicism, a distinctive feature of multinucleate fungi in which individual nuclei harbour divergent chromosomal complements. More broadly, my current research focuses on understanding how interactions between transposable elements and genome defense mechanisms have shaped genome evolution in plant pathogens.
